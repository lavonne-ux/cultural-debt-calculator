What to Consult On
Your entry point: The VP has seen their dollar figure. Your job in the consultation is to move from "here's your number" to "here's exactly where it's coming from and what we do about it." Each of the six drivers below is a distinct consulting conversation — and a distinct revenue opportunity. Lead with the largest cost driver first — that is always Manager Capability Gap.
1
Manager Capability Gap
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "What percentage of your managers have received formal leadership development in the last 12 months?" — silence answers the question
Lead with the number: 70% of engagement variance comes directly from the manager — Gallup 2025
Frame: "This isn't a talent problem — it's a capability infrastructure problem. Your managers were promoted for technical skills, not people leadership."
Connect: Every other debt driver on this page is made worse by a manager who lacks the capability to address it
Sources: Gallup State of the Global Workplace 2025; Gallup State of the American Manager 2015; McKinsey Organizational Health Index
Mitigation Plan
Manager capability assessment — baseline by team within 30 days
Leadership development intensive — 4-session program for engineering managers
Manager accountability framework — quarterly capability reviews tied to team performance metrics
Coaching program — 1:1 executive coaching for highest-leverage managers
2
Voice Suppression
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "When was the last time a Developer or Engineer changed a senior architect's decision?" — silence tells you everything
Google Project Aristotle 2016: psychological safety is the #1 predictor of engineering team performance — above talent, seniority, or team composition
Frame: "Bugs that aren't reported early cost 5–10× more to fix in production. Voice suppression is a quality control failure, not just a culture problem."
Connect: This is your innovation pipeline — and it's currently blocked at the source
Sources: Google Project Aristotle 2016; Amy Edmondson The Fearless Organization (HBS); DORA State of DevOps 2019
Mitigation Plan
Psychological safety baseline measurement — by team, not organization-wide
Challenger safety training — structured dissent protocols for Engineers and Developers
"Pre-mortem" practice built into sprint planning — formally surfaces concerns before launch
Senior engineer accountability — responses to challenges tracked quarterly
3
Failure Punishment Reflex
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "How does your team respond when a sprint fails or a deployment breaks production?" — the answer reveals the culture instantly
McKinsey: punitive cultures lose 20–30% of potential productivity; teams stop taking the risks that produce breakthroughs
Frame: "R&D and ML teams cannot produce innovation in a punitive environment. Risk-taking is the job — punishment kills the output."
Connect: Every failed experiment that goes unreported is institutional knowledge that disappears
Sources: McKinsey Global Institute; Amy Edmondson Right Kind of Wrong HBS 2023; Ed Catmull Creativity Inc (Pixar)
Mitigation Plan
Failure culture audit — structured review of last 6 months of incident responses
Blameless post-mortem protocol — implemented across all engineering teams
Innovation safety framework — defined risk tolerance by team type
Leadership modeling — VP demonstrates failure acceptance publicly within 30 days
4
Communication Lag & Feedback Debt
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "What percentage of your 1:1s end with a specific measurable action item for the engineer?" — most VPs don't know
Work Institute 2020: 77% of voluntary turnover is preventable — and feedback lag is a primary driver of avoidable exits
Frame: "Feedback delay is not kindness — it is operational inefficiency. Engineers optimize for the wrong output every sprint cycle it goes uncorrected."
Connect: Every late feedback conversation is a rework event waiting to happen
Sources: Work Institute Retention Report 2020; Kim Scott Radical Candor 2017; Marcus Buckingham Nine Lies About Work 2019
Mitigation Plan
SBI Feedback training — Situation, Behavior, Impact framework for all engineering managers
30-day feedback cadence mandate — no engineer goes more than 30 days without a clear performance signal
Sprint retro redesign — structured prompts requiring specific behavioral observations
Exit interview redesign — trace the feedback timeline for every departure
5
Brilliant Jerk Immunity
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "Do you have any high performers whose behavior you've been managing around rather than addressing directly?" — the pause before the answer is your data
HBS Dylan Minor 2015: one toxic employee costs the organization more than twice the value of a superstar hire
Frame: "Tolerated toxicity is a choice with a price tag. The cost of keeping this person is greater than the cost of losing their output."
Porath: de-energizing relationships have 4–7× stronger negative impact than positive relationships have positive impact
Sources: Dylan Minor HBS Toxic Workers 2015; Christine Porath Georgetown 2022; Netflix Culture Deck Reed Hastings
Mitigation Plan
Tolerated toxicity audit — identify and document all instances within 30 days
Behavioral accountability protocol — clear documented expectations with consequences
Performance separation framework — decouple technical output from behavioral standards
Leadership coaching — VP support through the difficult conversation process
6
Shadow Management
—
Consultation Talking Points
Ask: "How much of your week is spent managing interpersonal dynamics, conflict, or culture issues that shouldn't require your attention?"
Harvard CEO Study 2018: 36% of leader time spent in reactive mode — handling fires that should have been managed at lower levels
Frame: "This is the closing argument. Every dollar on this report flows upward and lands on your desk. Shadow management is what that cost looks like in your calendar."
Direct salary sunk cost: $90K–$144K of VP annual compensation spent on cultural debt maintenance instead of innovation — Harvard/Accountemps research
Sources: Harvard CEO Study 2018; Accountemps Survey ERE.net 2011; CultureAlly 2026; Myers-Briggs workplace conflict research
Mitigation Plan
VP time audit — document actual hours spent on cultural debt maintenance for 30 days
Escalation protocol redesign — what should and should not reach VP level
Manager accountability structure — middle management empowered to resolve cultural friction independently
Quarterly Cultural Debt Index™ — track VP time reclaimed as intervention ROI metric

Objection Handling
Q"We already have an HR team handling this."
HR manages compliance. Cultural debt is an operational performance problem — it lives in sprint velocity, code quality, and attrition rates, not HR policy. This engagement belongs in engineering leadership, not HR.
Q"I'm not sure our team has a culture problem."
That's exactly what the data showed. The numbers came from your inputs. The calculator uses industry-validated research multipliers — the percentages you entered produced this cost. The question isn't whether the problem exists. It's whether you want to recover the cost.
Q"What's the ROI on your engagement?"
Your assessment just showed a 7-figure cultural debt cost. A full engagement runs $25,000–$40,000. Organizations that implement with fidelity see 15–25% reduction in voluntary attrition and measurable sprint velocity gains within two quarters. The math is straightforward.
Q"Can we just do a one-time training?"
One-time training addresses awareness, not behavior. Cultural debt is accumulated over months or years — a single session does not reverse compounding patterns. The assessment engagement is the right entry point: low investment, high clarity, clear path forward.
Q"Our high performer is too valuable to lose."
HBS research shows one toxic employee costs more than twice the value of a superstar hire when you account for the people they drive out. The question is not whether you can afford to address it — it's whether you can afford not to. The number on this report includes that cost.
